About COVID-19

COVID-19 is a viral respiratory illness caused by the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2. Since its emergence in late 2019, the pandemic has had significant impacts on global health, economies, and daily life. This section provides information on prevention strategies, common symptoms, and treatment options for COVID-19. Select a state to view graphs and data related to the pandemic in your area.

Prevention

  • Getting vaccinated
  • Wearing masks in public settings
  • Practicing good hand hygiene
  • Maintaining physical distance from others, especially in crowded or indoor areas.
  • Following public health guidelines (quarantine and isolation protocols)

Symptoms

COVID-19 symptoms can range from mild to severe and typically appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus. Common symptoms include:

  • Fever or chills
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Muscle or body aches
  • Headache
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Congestion or runny nose
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea

It is crucial to seek medical attention if you are experiencing persistent chest pain, confusion, inability to wake or stay awake, or bluish lips or face.

Treatment

There is no specific antiviral treatment for COVID-19. For mild cases, individuals can often manage symptoms at home with rest, hydration, and over-the-counter medications. In more severe cases, hospitalization may be necessary to provide oxygen therapy, medications, and other supportive care. It is essential to consult with healthcare professionals for personalized advice and to follow their recommendations for managing the illness.
